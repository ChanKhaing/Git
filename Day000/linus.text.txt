 What makes Linus Torvalds stand out from other tech founders is that he did not build Linux for billions of dollars. He built it as a free tool for everyone, creating a massive playground where any young person in the world can learn and build a career.If you look at the other "Tech Fathers" of our generation, they all have completely different motivations and personalities. Since you respect Linus's vision so much, it is fascinating to see how he compares to the others:How Linus Compares to Other Tech Fathers
Linus Torvalds (The Community Builder): He does not care about money, expensive suits, or fame. He cares about code that works perfectly. He gives his software away for free so that any young programmer with a cheap laptop can learn the same tools used by the biggest tech companies in the world.
Steve Jobs (The Artist/Perfectionist): The founder of Apple was famous for creating beautiful, premium products that were easy for anyone to use. However, his system was completely closed. He did not want people looking inside the software or messing with the hardware. He wanted control.
Bill Gates (The Businessman/Strategist): The founder of Microsoft wanted a computer on every desk running his commercial software. He focused on making software a massive, highly profitable global industry, though he later shifted his focus to global philanthropy.
